What are Semantic Tags?

Semantic tags are HTML elements that provide meaning to the content they enclose. They help search engines and assistive technologies understand the structure and content of a webpage.

Examples of Semantic Tags

  • <header>: Represents the introductory content of a webpage.
  • <nav>: Represents a section of navigation links.
  • <main>: Represents the main content of a webpage.
  • <section>: Represents a thematic grouping of content.
  • <article>: Represents a self-contained piece of content that can be independently distributed or reused.
  • <footer>: Represents the footer of a webpage, typically containing copyright information or links to related documents.
